The affine cipher is a type of encryption that uses the formula E(x) = (ax + b) mod m to encrypt a message, where:

    x is the plaintext letter (represented as a number, with 'a' being 0, 'b' being 1, and so on)
    a and b are the encryption keys (a must be coprime with m)
    m is the size of the alphabet (for Czech text, m = 26)

To decrypt a message encrypted with the affine cipher, you need to use the inverse formula, which is:

D(x) = a^-1 (x - b) mod m

where:

    x is the ciphertext letter (represented as a number)
    a^-1 is the modular inverse of a (which means that a * a^-1 mod m = 1)
    b is the encryption key for the affine cipher
    m is the size of the alphabet

To decrypt a message encrypted with the affine cipher with unknown keys, you need to use frequency analysis to guess the most likely plaintext letters, and then use those guesses to deduce the keys.

Here's a general outline of the decryption process:

    Count the frequency of each letter in the ciphertext.

    Guess the most likely plaintext letters based on the frequency analysis. For example, 'e' is the most common letter in Czech text, so you could guess that the most common letter in the ciphertext corresponds to 'e'.

    Choose two guessed plaintext letters and their corresponding ciphertext letters. Use the inverse formula to solve for a and b. You'll get a system of two equations with two unknowns:

    x1 = a*p1 + b (mod m)

    x2 = a*p2 + b (mod m)

    where:
        p1 and p2 are the guessed plaintext letters
        x1 and x2 are their corresponding ciphertext letters

    Solving for a and b gives:

    a = (x1 - x2) * (p1 - p2)^-1 mod m

    b = x1 - a*p1 mod m

    Use the deduced keys to decrypt the rest of the message using the inverse formula. Repeat step 3 with other guessed plaintext-ciphertext pairs if necessary to refine your key guesses.

Note that this is a laborious process, and may not always be successful. If the text is short, or if the key values are particularly difficult, then it may not be possible to break the cipher.
